**Power Strategies**
**🔁 The "Teach-Back" trick with Claude:** After studying a region, close your notes and tell Claude: *"I'm going to explain the anatomy of the axilla to you. Correct me if I'm wrong."* This forces active recall and Claude catches gaps.
**📍 Clinical anchoring:** For every structure, ask: *"What happens if this is damaged?"* Claude can give you instant clinical correlations. This makes structures memorable and exam-relevant.
**🗺️ Draw it out:** Once per region, close everything and draw the anatomy from memory (stick figures are fine). Then check against Complete Anatomy. This is brutally effective.
