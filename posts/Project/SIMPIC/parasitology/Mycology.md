Mycology
Sunday, March 29, 2026
1:27 AM

Medically important fungi are traditionally categorized by the type and depth of tissue infection they cause. Below is a structured list of key fungi alongside the specific **trigger words** and **diagnostic clues** frequently encountered in competition questions like USMLE and SIMPIC.
**I. Superficial Mycoses**
These infections are confined to the outermost layers of the skin and hair.
- ***Malassezia furfur* (Pityriasis versicolor)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Spaghetti and meatballs appearance, lipophilic, hypopigmented or hyperpigmented patches, dandruff, neonates on IV lipid supplements.
  - **Diagnostic Clues:** KOH mount shows short, curved, septate hyphae and yeast-like cells; cultures require olive oil.
- ***Hortaea werneckii* (Tinea nigra)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Dark, melanoma-like lesions on palms or soles; dematiaceous (pigmented).
- ***Trichosporon* species (White Piedra)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Soft, pasty white/brown swellings on hair shaft; arthroconidia and blastoconidia.

**II. Cutaneous Mycoses (Dermatophytoses)**
Infections involving the deeper layers of the epidermis, hair, and nails.
- **The Dermatophytes (*Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton*)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Ringworm, tinea (capitis, corporis, pedis, etc.), circular scaling lesion, central clearing, hair loss, athlete’s foot, jock itch.
  - **Diagnostic Clues:** Arthroconidia, septate hyphae, Wood’s lamp (UV) fluorescence (especially for *Microsporum*).
  - **Specific Genus Clues:**
    - ***Microsporum*:** Numerous thick-walled, rough macroconidia.
    - ***Trichophyton*:** Numerous microconidia (teardrop/peg-shaped); *T. rubrum* is the most common cause of nail infections.
    - ***Epidermophyton*:** Smooth-walled macroconidia in clusters; no microconidia.

**III. Subcutaneous Mycoses**
Infections introduced by traumatic inoculation into deep skin layers.
- ***Sporothrix schenckii* (Sporotrichosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Rose gardener’s disease, thorn prick, sphagnum moss, cigar-shaped yeast, lymphocutaneous spread (nodules along lymphatic drainage).
  - **Diagnostic Clues:** Thermally dimorphic; "daisy petal" conidia at 25°C.
- **Agents of Chromoblastomycosis (e.g., *Fonsecaea*, *Phialophora*)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Cauliflower-like lesions, sclerotic bodies (copper pennies), muriform cells, dematiaceous.
- **Agents of Eumycotic Mycetoma (e.g., *Madurella*, *Pseudallescheria boydii*)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Grains (granules), sinus tracts, chronic swelling (tumefaction), "sulfur granules" (clinical overlap with *Actinomyces* bacteria).

**IV. Systemic Mycoses (Dimorphic Pathogens)**
True pathogens that cause disease in both healthy and compromised hosts and are geographically restricted.
- ***Histoplasma capsulatum* (Histoplasmosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Bird or bat droppings (guano), bat caves, chicken coops, Mississippi/Ohio River Valleys, intracellular yeast in macrophages.
  - **Diagnostic Clues:** Tuberculate macroconidia at 25°C.
- ***Coccidioides immitis/posadasii* (Coccidioidomycosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Valley Fever, San Joaquin Valley, desert sand, barrel-shaped arthroconidia, spherules with endospores.
- ***Blastomyces dermatitidis* (Blastomycosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Broad-based budding yeast, double retractile wall, Great Lakes, Mississippi River Valley, rotting wood/organic matter.
- ***Paracoccidioides brasiliensis* (Paracoccidioidomycosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Pilot’s wheel (captain's wheel), mariner’s wheel appearance, multiple budding, South America/Latin America.
- ***Talaromyces marneffei* (Penicilliosis)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Bamboo rats, Southeast Asia, diffusible red pigment in culture, binary fission yeast.

**V. Opportunistic Mycoses**
Ubiquitous fungi that primarily cause life-threatening disease in immunocompromised hosts.
- ***Candida albicans***
  - **Trigger Words:** Thrush, cottage cheese discharge, pseudohyphae, germ tube, AIDS (CD4 \< 200), satellite lesions, diaper rash.
- ***Cryptococcus neoformans/gattii***
  - **Trigger Words:** Polysaccharide capsule, India ink, pigeon droppings, latex agglutination, soap bubble lesions in brain, AIDS (CD4 \< 100), neurotropic.
- ***Aspergillus* species**
  - **Trigger Words:** Septate hyphae, dichotomous branching at acute (45°) angles, aspergilloma (fungus ball), neutropenia, allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis (ABPA), galactomannan.
- **Mucormycetes (*Rhizopus, Mucor*)**
  - **Trigger Words:** Nonseptate (aseptate) hyphae, broad/ribbon-like, right-angle branching, diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), rhinocerebral infection, black eschar.
- ***Pneumocystis jirovecii***
  - **Trigger Words:** Ground-glass appearance on X-ray, cup-shaped cysts, silver stain (GMS), disk-shaped, AIDS (CD4 \< 200), trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole prophylaxis.
- ***Fusarium* species**
  - **Trigger Words:** Fungal keratitis, contact lenses, positive blood cultures (unlike *Aspergillus*), sickle-shaped macroconidia.

**VI. Unusual or Emerging Fungi**
- ***Pneumocystis jirovecii***: Now classified as a fungus (formerly protozoan); cannot be grown in vitro.
- ***Lacazia loboi***: Keloid-like lesions, chains of yeast cells, associated with dolphins.
- ***Adiaspiromyces crescens***: Adiaconidia (massive enlargement without replication), pulmonary nodules.
