The ERBB2 (HER2) receptor forms dimer-dependent, active signaling complexes primarily through its extracellular Domain II, specifically a 12-residue loop (residues 252-257) that acts as a dimerization arm. The transmembrane (TM) domain also plays a critical role in dimerization.

Key ErbB2 Dimer-Forming Epitopes and Domains:
Extracellular Domain II (Domain II, 252-257): This region forms a
-hairpin that protrudes to facilitate "back-to-head" dimerization. Pertuzumab (2C4) targets this area, inhibiting HER2 heterodimerization.
Transmembrane (TM) Domain: The TM domain acts as a stable dimerization interface (residues around V664) and is critical for activation.
Domain III (474T—R477P478): A specific epitope in domain III is involved in interactions that, when targeted by monoclonal antibodies, can inhibit dimerization.
