Bacteria
Sunday, March 29, 2026
1:20 AM

To succeed in medical microbiology competitions like SIMPIC, you should know the following bacteria, categorized by their structural and staining properties. The accompanying **trigger words** are high-yield clinical and laboratory clues frequently used to identify these organisms in question stems.
**I. Major Recurring Species (High-Yield)**
These seven species are involved in multiple organ systems and are critical to master first.
- **Staphylococcus aureus:** Grapelike clusters, catalase-positive, **coagulase-positive**, golden colonies, protein A, superantigens (TSST-1), MRSA.
- **Streptococcus pyogenes (Group A): Group A**, pharyngitis, pyoderma, **rheumatic fever**, glomerulonephritis, bacitracin-sensitive, PYR-positive.
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae: Lancet-shaped diplococci**, alpha-hemolytic, **quellung reaction**, **optochin-sensitive**, bile-soluble, polysaccharide capsule.
- **Escherichia coli:** Lactose-fermenting, indole-positive, **UTI**, neonatal meningitis, gastroenteritis (ETEC, EPEC, EAEC, STEC/EHEC, EIEC).
- **Pseudomonas aeruginosa: Pyocyanin (blue-green pigment)**, fruity/grapelike odor, oxidase-positive, opportunistic, **burn patients**, cystic fibrosis.
- **Haemophilus influenzae: Coccobacilli**, requires **X and V factors**, chocolate agar, **epiglottitis**, type b (PRP capsule).
- **Chlamydia trachomatis:** Obligate intracellular, **elementary and reticulate bodies**, trachoma, LGV, non-Gram staining.

**II. Gram-Positive Bacteria**
**1. Gram-Positive Cocci**
- **Staphylococcus epidermidis:** Coagulase-negative, **slime layer**, opportunistic, prosthetic devices/catheters.
- **Staphylococcus saprophyticus:** Coagulase-negative, **UTIs in newly sexually active young women**.
- **Streptococcus agalactiae (Group B): Group B**, **neonatal sepsis/meningitis**, screening pregnant women, CAMP test positive.
- **Enterococcus (e.g., E. faecalis): Bile-resistant**, grows in 6.5% NaCl, PYR-positive, high-level drug resistance (VRE).
- **Viridans Streptococci:** Alpha-hemolytic, **bile-insoluble**, optochin-resistant, dental caries, subacute endocarditis.
**2. Gram-Positive Rods (Spore-Forming)**
- **Bacillus anthracis: Medusa head colonies**, nonmotile, polypeptide capsule, **edema and lethal toxins**, wool-sorter’s disease.
- **Bacillus cereus: Fried rice**, emetic and diarrheal forms, traumatic eye infections.
- **Clostridium tetani:** Anaerobic, **terminal spores (drumstick)**, lockjaw, spastic paralysis, **tetanospasmin**.
- **Clostridium botulinum:** Anaerobic, **flaccid paralysis**, honey (infants), preformed toxin in canned goods.
- **Clostridium perfringens: Gas gangrene**, myonecrosis, double zone of hemolysis, **alpha-toxin (lecithinase)**.
- **Clostridium difficile: Pseudomembranous colitis**, toxins A and B, antibiotic-associated.
**3. Gram-Positive Rods (Non-Spore-Forming)**
- **Listeria monocytogenes: Tumbling motility**, cold growth, actin rockets, deli meats/soft cheeses, neonatal meningitis.
- **Corynebacterium diphtheriae: Club-shaped**, **Chinese letters**, pseudomembrane, diphtheria toxin (inhibits EF-2).
- **Erysipelothrix rhusiopathiae: Fishmongers/butchers**, erysipeloid (dark red skin lesion), H2S production.

**III. Gram-Negative Bacteria**
**1. Gram-Negative Cocci/Diplococci**
- **Neisseria gonorrhoeae:** Intracellular, kidney-bean shaped, **Thayer-Martin medium**, glucose-only metabolism.
- **Neisseria meningitidis:** Capsule, **maltose and glucose fermentation**, Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome.
- **Moraxella catarrhalis:** Oxidase-positive, **COPD exacerbations**, otitis media.
**2. Gram-Negative Rods (Enterobacteriaceae)**
- **Salmonella Typhi: Rose spots**, H2S-positive, motile, typhoid fever, gallbladder carrier state.
- **Shigella: Non-motile**, low infectious dose, Shiga toxin, **bacillary dysentery**.
- **Klebsiella pneumoniae: Currant jelly sputum**, mucoid colonies, large capsule, alcoholism.
- **Proteus mirabilis: Swarming motility**, **urease-positive**, staghorn calculi (alkaline urine).
- **Yersinia pestis: Bipolar staining (safety pin)**, plague, fleas/rodents.
**3. Other Gram-Negative Rods**
- **Vibrio cholerae: Comma-shaped**, **rice-water stools**, TCBS agar, cholera toxin (cAMP).
- **Campylobacter jejuni: Seagull wing appearance**, 42°C growth, microaerophilic, **Guillain-Barré syndrome**.
- **Helicobacter pylori: Potent urease**, **gastric ulcers**, gastric adenocarcinoma/MALT lymphoma.
- **Legionella pneumophila:** Charcoal agar (BCYE), **silver stain**, air conditioning/water cooling towers, hyponatremia.
- **Bordetella pertussis: Whooping cough**, paroxysmal stage, pertussis toxin, Regan-Lowe medium.
- **Francisella tularensis:** Tularemia, **rabbits/ticks**, very low infectious dose (biohazard).

**IV. Unique Morphologies & Staining**
**1. Acid-Fast Bacteria**
- **Mycobacterium tuberculosis: Acid-fast**, **mycolic acids**, cord factor, PPD, night sweats/weight loss.
- **Mycobacterium leprae:** Leprosy, armadillo reservoir, grows in cool tissues.
- **Nocardia:** Weakly acid-fast, **branching filamentous**, aerobic, brain abscess in immunocompromised.
- **Actinomyces israelii: Sulfur granules**, anaerobic, branching filamentous, molar tooth colonies.
**2. Spirochetes**
- **Treponema pallidum:** Syphilis, **dark-field microscopy**, non-culturable, painless chancre.
- **Borrelia burgdorferi: Lyme disease**, **Ixodes tick**, erythema migrans (bull's eye rash).
- **Leptospira interrogans: Hooked ends**, animal urine (rats/dogs), Weil disease.
**3. Cell Wall-Less & Intracellular**
- **Mycoplasma pneumoniae: No cell wall**, sterols in membrane, **"fried egg" colonies**, cold agglutinins.
- **Rickettsia rickettsii: Rocky Mountain spotted fever**, **tick**, vasculitis, centripetal rash (palms/soles).
- **Coxiella burnetii: Q fever**, cattle/sheep parturition, no rash.

Bacterial toxins are categorized into two primary groups: **endotoxin**, which is an integral part of the Gram-negative cell wall, and **exotoxins**, which are secreted proteins. Below is a structured summary of the toxins you should know, along with the "trigger words" frequently encountered in competition questions.
**I. Endotoxin (Lipopolysaccharide - LPS)**
Endotoxin consists of the **lipid A** component of the Gram-negative cell envelope.
- **Mechanism:** Interacts with CD14 and TLR4 on macrophages to trigger a **cytokine storm** (IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α), activating complement and coagulation cascades.
- **Trigger Words:** Lipid A, **Gram-negative sepsis**, fever, **DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation)**, shock, Limulus test.

**II. Superantigen Exotoxins (Type I Toxins)**
These toxins bind directly to MHC II and T-cell receptors without requiring antigen processing, leading to massive T-cell activation.
- **Toxic Shock Syndrome Toxin-1 (TSST-1):** Produced by *S. aureus*.
  - **Trigger Words:** High-absorbency **tampons**, diffuse red rash that **desquamates** (palms and soles), multisystem organ failure.
- **Staphylococcal Enterotoxin:** Produced by *S. aureus*.
  - **Trigger Words: Food poisoning**, rapid onset (1–8 hours), **heat-stable**, vomiting (projectile), potato salad/custard.
- **Streptococcal Pyrogenic Exotoxins (SPE A & C):** Produced by *S. pyogenes*.
  - **Trigger Words: Scarlet fever**, **sandpaper rash**, **strawberry tongue**, "flesh-eating" bacteria.

**III. Membrane-Disrupting Toxins (Type II Toxins)**
These toxins form pores in cell membranes or enzymatically degrade lipids.
- **Alpha-toxin (Lecithinase):** Produced by *Clostridium perfringens*.
  - **Trigger Words: Gas gangrene**, myonecrosis, **Nagler test**, double zone of hemolysis, phospholipase C.
- **Streptolysin O:** Produced by *S. pyogenes*.
  - **Trigger Words: ASO titer** (marker of recent infection), oxygen-labile, highly antigenic, pore-forming.
- **P-V Leukocidin (PVL):** Produced by *S. aureus*.
  - **Trigger Words:** Lyses WBCs, **CA-MRSA**, severe necrotic pneumonia, cutaneous abscesses.

**IV. Protein Synthesis Inhibitors (A-B Subunit Toxins)**
The B subunit binds to host cells, while the A subunit provides toxic enzymatic activity.
- **Diphtheria Toxin:** Produced by *Corynebacterium diphtheriae*.
  - **Mechanism:** ADP-ribosylates **Elongation Factor-2 (EF-2)**.
  - **Trigger Words: Pseudomembrane**, Chinese characters, **Elek test**, myocarditis, bull neck.
- **Exotoxin A:** Produced by *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*.
  - **Mechanism:** Identical to diphtheria toxin (inhibits EF-2).
  - **Trigger Words: Burn patients**, cystic fibrosis, pyocyanin (blue-green pigment), opportunistic.
- **Shiga Toxin / Shiga-like Toxin (Vero Toxin):** Produced by *Shigella dysenteriae* and *STEC (E. coli O157:H7)*.
  - **Mechanism:** Inactivates the **60S ribosomal subunit**.
  - **Trigger Words: HUS (Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome)**, bloody diarrhea, undercooked hamburger, Gb3 receptor.

**V. cAMP/cGMP Disrupting Toxins (Secretory Toxins)**
- **Cholera Toxin:** Produced by *Vibrio cholerae*.
  - **Mechanism:** ADP-ribosylates G-protein, increasing **cAMP**.
  - **Trigger Words: Rice-water stools**, comma-shaped, **GM1 ganglioside receptor**, TCBS agar.
- **Heat-Labile (LT) & Heat-Stable (ST) Toxins:** Produced by *ETEC*.
  - **Mechanism:** LT increases **cAMP** (like cholera toxin); ST increases **cGMP**.
  - **Trigger Words: Traveler's diarrhea**, small intestine action, watery diarrhea.
- **Anthrax Toxin:** Produced by *Bacillus anthracis*.
  - **Mechanism:** PA (Protective Antigen) + **EF (Edema Factor: adenylate cyclase)** or **LF (Lethal Factor: MAPKK protease)**.
  - **Trigger Words: Woolsorter’s disease**, Medusa head, **black eschar**, PA + EF + LF.
- **Pertussis Toxin:** Produced by *Bordetella pertussis*.
  - **Mechanism:** Increases cAMP by disabling inhibitory G-protein.
  - **Trigger Words: Whooping cough**, paroxysmal stage, profound **lymphocytosis**.

**VI. Neurotoxins**
- **Tetanospasmin:** Produced by *Clostridium tetani*.
  - **Mechanism:** Cleaves SNARE proteins; blocks release of **inhibitory neurotransmitters (GABA/Glycine)**.
  - **Trigger Words: Spastic paralysis**, lockjaw (trismus), **risus sardonicus**, opisthotonos.
- **Botulinum Toxin:** Produced by *Clostridium botulinum*.
  - **Mechanism:** Cleaves SNARE proteins; blocks release of **Acetylcholine** at neuromuscular junctions.
  - **Trigger Words: Flaccid paralysis**, "floppy baby," **honey** (infants), canned goods, diplopia (double vision).

For medical microbiology competitions like **SIMPIC**, it is essential to categorize antibiotics by their **mechanism of action** and **resistance patterns** while using only **generic names**. The following list provides a structured overview of high-yield antibiotics and the **trigger words** frequently used in competition scenarios.
**I. Cell Wall Synthesis Inhibitors (Bactericidal)**
These drugs are generally less toxic to humans because they target **peptidoglycan**, a structure absent in mammalian cells.
- **Penicillins:**
  - **Penicillin G & V:** Trigger words include **Natural**, **Syphilis** (T. pallidum), and **Susceptible Streptococci**.
  - **Nafcillin & Oxacillin:** Trigger words include **Antistaphylococcal**, **MSSA**, and **\$\beta\$-lactamase resistant**.
  - **Ampicillin & Amoxicillin:** Trigger words include **Broad-spectrum**, **Enterococci** (inhibited but not killed), and **Listeria**.
  - **Piperacillin:** Trigger words include **Antipseudomonal** and **Ureidopenicillin**.
- **Cephalosporins:**
  - **Cefazolin (1st Gen):** Trigger words include **Surgical prophylaxis** and **Gram-positive cocci**.
  - **Cefoxitin & Cefotetan (2nd Gen):** Trigger words include **Cephamycins**, **Anaerobes**, and **Bacteroides fragilis**.
  - **Ceftriaxone (3rd Gen):** Trigger words include **Meningitis** (crosses blood-brain barrier), **Gonorrhea**, and **Lyme disease** (late stage).
  - **Ceftazidime (3rd Gen):** Trigger words include **Pseudomonas** activity and **Melioidosis**.
  - **Cefepime (4th Gen):** Trigger words include **Extended-spectrum** and **Gram-negative rods**.
  - **Ceftaroline:** Trigger words include **MRSA-active cephalosporin**.
- **Carbapenems (Imipenem, Meropenem, Ertapenem):** Trigger words include **Very broad-spectrum**, **Seizures** (Imipenem side effect), and **ESBL-producing Enterobacteriaceae**.
- **Glycopeptides (Vancomycin):** Trigger words include **MRSA**, **D-Ala-D-Ala**, **Oral use for C. difficile**, **Red man syndrome**, and **VRE** (resistance).
**II. Protein Synthesis Inhibitors**
These drugs target the bacterial **70S ribosome** (30S or 50S subunits).
- **30S Subunit Inhibitors:**
  - **Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Amikacin):** Trigger words include **Bactericidal** (unique for protein inhibitors), **\$O_2\$ dependent** (not for anaerobes), **Nephrotoxicity**, **Ototoxicity**, and **Synergy** with \$\beta\$-lactams.
  - **Tetracyclines (Doxycycline, Minocycline):** Trigger words include **Bacteriostatic**, **Teeth staining** (children), **Rickettsia/Chlamydia**, **Lyme disease**, and **Acne**.
- **50S Subunit Inhibitors:**
  - **Macrolides (Erythromycin, Azithromycin, Clarithromycin):** Trigger words include **Penicillin allergy**, **Legionella**, **Mycoplasma**, **Campylobacter**, and **23S rRNA methylation** (resistance).
  - **Clindamycin:** Trigger words include **Anaerobes**, **Infection above the diaphragm**, **MRSA (community-acquired)**, and **C. difficile colitis** (risk factor).
  - **Linezolid (Oxazolidinone):** Trigger words include **VRE**, **MRSA**, and **Thrombocytopenia**.
  - **Chloramphenicol:** Trigger words include **Gray baby syndrome**, **Aplastic anemia**, and **Salmonella** (historic use).
**III. Nucleic Acid Synthesis Inhibitors (Bactericidal)**
- **Fluoroquinolones (Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin):** Trigger words include **DNA gyrase**, **Topoisomerase IV**, **UTI**, **Pseudomonas** (Ciprofloxacin), and **Anthrax**.
- **Metronidazole:** Trigger words include **Anaerobes**, **\$B. fragilis\$**, **C. difficile**, **Protozoa** (Trichomonas/Giardia), and **Disruption of DNA**.
- **Rifampin:** Trigger words include **RNA polymerase**, **Tuberculosis**, **Meningococcal prophylaxis**, and **Orange-colored fluids**.
**IV. Antimetabolites (Folic Acid Inhibitors)**
- **Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX):** Trigger words include **PABA analog**, **Dihydrofolate reductase**, **Pneumocystis** (PCP), **Nocardia**, and **UTI**.
**V. Cell Membrane Disruptors**
- **Daptomycin:** Trigger words include **Cyclic lipopeptide**, **Membrane depolarization**, and **Vancomycin-resistant Gram-positives**.
- **Polymyxins (Colistin):** Trigger words include **Detergent-like**, **Nephrotoxicity**, and **Last-resort for MDR Gram-negatives**.
**VI. Key Resistance Mechanism Clues**
- **mecA gene:** Trigger word for **MRSA** (altered PBP2a).
- **vanA gene:** Trigger word for **VRE** or **VRSA**.
- **ESBL:** Clue for resistance to **penicillins and cephalosporins** in Enterobacteriaceae; requires **carbapenems**.
- **KPC/NDM-1:** Clue for **Carbapenem resistance**.
