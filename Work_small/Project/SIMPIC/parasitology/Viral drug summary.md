Viral drug summary
Wednesday, March 11, 2026
4:22 PM

Antiviral drugs are categorized based on which stage of the viral replication cycle they inhibit, and their names often contain specific **suffixes or word parts** that indicate their pharmacological class.
**1. Protease Inhibitors (PIs)**
These drugs block the viral protease enzyme, which is essential for cleaving polyproteins into functional units for mature virion assembly.
- **Naming Clue:** Names typically end in **"-navir"** (derived from "no virus").
- **Examples:** Saquinavir, ritonavir, indinavir, and darunavir (used for HIV). For **Hepatitis C (HCV)**, names often end in **"-previr"** such as simeprevir, boceprevir, and telaprevir.
**2. Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitors (INSTIs)**
These medications prevent the viral integrase enzyme from inserting the viral genome into the host cell's DNA.
- **Naming Clue:** Names typically contain **"-tegravir"**.
- **Examples:** Raltegravir, dolutegravir, and bictegravir.
**3. Neuraminidase Inhibitors**
Used primarily for influenza A and B, these drugs block the neuraminidase enzyme, preventing the release (budding) of new virus particles from the host cell.
- **Naming Clue:** Names typically end in **"-mivir"**.
- **Examples:** Oseltamivir (Tamiflu), zanamivir, and peramivir.
**4. Nucleoside and Nucleotide Analogs**
These are structural mimics of the building blocks of DNA/RNA. They work by competing for the active site of viral polymerases and acting as **chain terminators** to stop genome replication.
- **Naming Clues:** Many anti-herpetic drugs end in **"-cyclovir"** or **"-ciclovir"**. Anti-HIV versions often have abbreviations like **"-vudine"** or **"-ine"**.
- **Examples:** Acyclovir, valacyclovir, and ganciclovir (Herpes/CMV). Zidovudine (AZT) and lamivudine (3TC) (HIV).
**5. Entry and Fusion Inhibitors**
These drugs prevent the virus from attaching to or entering the host cell by blocking receptors or fusion proteins.
- **Naming Clue:** CCR5 coreceptor antagonists often end in **"-viroc"**.
- **Examples: Maraviroc** (entry inhibitor) and **Enfuvirtide** (T20, a fusion inhibitor).
**6. Biologic Agents (Monoclonal Antibodies)**
Some antiviral therapies utilize lab-created antibodies to neutralize extracellular virions.
- **Naming Clue:** Names end in **"-mab"**, with specific prefixes indicating the source:
  - **-omab:** Fully mouse/murine sequences.
  - **-ximab:** Chimeric (part mouse, part human).
  - **-zumab:** Humanized (mostly human).
  - **-umab:** Fully human sequences.
- **Example:** Palivizumab (used for Respiratory Syncytial Virus/RSV).
