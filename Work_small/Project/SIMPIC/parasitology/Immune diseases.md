Immune diseases
Sunday, March 29, 2026
1:27 AM

A structured list of immune diseases and their associated "trigger words" used in medical competitions (like SIMPIC) is provided below, based on the clinical cases and textbook descriptions in the sources.
**I. Hypersensitivity Reactions (Types I-IV)**
These disorders are defined by an inappropriate or overly zealous immune response to non-pathogenic antigens.
| **Disease** | **Category** | **Trigger Words / Competition Clues** |
|----|----|----|
| **Anaphylaxis / Hives** | **Type I (IgE)** | **Wheal and flare**, mast cell degranulation, **histamine**, bee/wasp venom, cardiovascular failure. |
| **Allergic Rhinitis / Hay Fever** | **Type I (IgE)** | **Rhinorrhea**, sneezing, **ragweed**, IL-4 mediated IgE synthesis, positive RAST test. |
| **Hemolytic Disease of the Newborn** | **Type II (Cytotoxic)** | **Rh-negative mother**, Rh-positive fetus, transplacental transfer of maternal IgG. |
| **Goodpasture’s Syndrome** | **Type II (Cytotoxic)** | **Anti-GBM** (glomerular basement membrane) antibodies, **Linear** deposition/fluorescent pattern, pulmonary hemorrhage. |
| **Serum Sickness** | **Type III (Immune Complex)** | **Foreign serum** injection, systemic reaction (fever, rash, joint pain), **transient** resolution after clearance. |
| **Arthus Reaction** | **Type III (Immune Complex)** | **Local** skin reaction, **hemorrhagic/necrotic lesions**, PMN infiltration. |
| **Contact Dermatitis** | **Type IV (Delayed)** | **Nickel**, **poison ivy** (pentadecacatechol), **rubber**, vesiculating lesion, erythema and induration after 3–5 days. |

**II. Autoimmune Diseases**
These diseases arise from a breakdown in self-tolerance, leading to the immune system attacking host tissues.
| **Disease** | **Target / Mechanism** | **Trigger Words / Competition Clues** |
|----|----|----|
| **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)** | Systemic; Ag-Ab complexes | **Butterfly rash**, **anti-dsDNA**, polyarthralgia, **lumpy bumpy** (random) pattern in kidney, low complement (C1q/C2/C4). |
| **Myasthenia Gravis** | Acetylcholine (ACh) receptor | **Muscle weakness and fatigue**, **Thymoma** or thymic hyperplasia, anti-ACh receptor antibody. |
| **Graves’ Disease** | TSH receptor (Agonist) | **Bulging eyes** (exophthalmos), chronically high thyroid hormones, rapid heartbeat. |
| **Hashimoto’s Thyroiditis** | Thyroid proteins | **Hypothyroidism**, TH1-cell reaction to thyroid antigens, self-limiting. |
| **Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)** | Joints; IgG-IgM complexes | **Rheumatoid factor** (Anti-IgG IgM), **Pannus** formation, subcutaneous nodules in joints. |
| **Sjögren’s Syndrome** | Salivary/lacrimal glands | **Dry eyes/mouth**, postmenopausal women, anti-ribonucleoprotein antigens. |
| **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)** | CNS myelin | **Demyelinating lesions (plaques)** in white matter, **increased IgG in spinal fluid**, relapsing course, anti-MBP. |

**III. Primary Immunodeficiency Diseases (PIDs)**
These are inherited genetic or developmental defects present at birth.
| **Disease** | **Defect** | **Trigger Words / Competition Clues** |
|----|----|----|
| **Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)** | T and B stem cells | **Absent thymus and tonsils**, **ADA** (adenosine deaminase) deficiency, **IL-2R gamma chain** mutation, fatal infancy. |
| **X-linked Agammaglobulinemia (Bruton's)** | **BTK** gene mutation | **Males**, absent B cells/germinal centers/tonsils, recurrent **pyogenic** (pus-forming) bacterial infections. |
| **DiGeorge Syndrome** | 3rd/4th pharyngeal pouches | **Hypocalcemia and tetany**, thymic aplasia, failure of T-cell development. |
| **Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome** | **WASP** protein; Cytoskeleton | **Triad: Thrombocytopenia (bleeding), eczema, recurrent infections**; poor response to capsular polysaccharides. |
| **Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)** | **NADPH oxidase** system | **Catalase-positive** infections (S. aureus, Aspergillus), **failed Nitroblue Tetrazolium (NBT) test**, treatment with IFN-γ. |
| **Selective IgA Deficiency** | Unknown (common) | **Most common PID**, **asymptomatic** or recurrent sinopulmonary infections, mucosal surface protection lost. |
| **Hyper IgM Syndrome** | **CD40L** (CD40 ligand) deficiency | High IgM, low/absent IgA/G/E, **failed isotype switching**, pyogenic infections. |
| **Chediak-Higashi Syndrome** | **LYST** protein defect | **Partial albinism**, **Giant granules** in neutrophils, inability to lyse bacteria. |
| **Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)** | **CD18** (integrin β2) defect | **Ulcerating skin infections without pus**, recurrent bacterial infections, impaired wound healing. |

**IV. Other Notable Immune-Related Clues**
- **Multiple Myeloma:** Malignant plasma cell clone, **Bence Jones proteins** (monoclonal light chains) in urine, excess IgG paraproteins.
- **AIDS: gp120/gp41**, **CD4 count \< 200/mm³**, **opportunistic infections** (Pneumocystis, CMV, Cryptosporidium), Western blot confirmation.
- **Ataxia Telangiectasia: Staggering gait**, vascular deformities, DNA repair defect, deficiency of T and B cells.
- **Burkitt’s Lymphoma:** Associated with **Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)** infection in malarial regions.
- **Chagas’ Disease:** Brazilian/South American, cardiomyopathy, transmitted by **reduviid bugs** (feces into bite site).
