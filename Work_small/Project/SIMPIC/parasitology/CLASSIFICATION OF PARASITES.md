CLASSIFICATION OF PARASITES
Monday, January 5, 2026
3:40 PM

## metazoan 
### Phylum Nemathelminthes
#### *Class Nematoda*
roundworms (body round in cross-section)
### Phylum Platyhelminthes
### flatworms
#### *Class Cestoda*
tapeworms (body flattened and segmented)
#### *Class Digenea*
trematodes, flukes (body flattened, leaf-shaped, and non-segmented)
## Protozoa
unicellular eukaryotic microorganisms.
### Phylum Sarcomastigophora
#### *Class Lobosea*
organisms that move by means of pseudopodia
#### *Class Zoomastigophorea*
organisms that move by means of flagella
### Phylum Ciliophora
#### *Class Kinetofragminophorea: organisms that*
move by means of cilia
### Phylum Apicomplexa
#### *Class Sporozoea*
Organisms with both sexual and asexual reproductive cycles; apical complex can be seen with an electron microscope
## Arthropods
possess a hard exoskeleton and jointed appendages. Only those that are parasitic to humans and those that transmit parasitic diseases are included.
### Phylum Arthropoda
#### *Class Insecta*
flies, mosquitoes, bugs, lice, fleas
#### *Class Arachnida*
ticks, mites

According to the provided sources, the following is a comprehensive list of parasites and their high-yield **trigger words**, clinical clues, and characteristic diagnostic findings frequently encountered in competition questions:
**Protozoa**
- \*\*\*Plasmodium spp.\*\*\*: **Malaria**, **quotidian**, **tertian**, **quartan**, **blackwater fever** (*P. falciparum*), **cerebral malaria**, **benign tertian** (*P. vivax*), **malignant tertian** (*P. falciparum*), **multiple ring forms** (*P. falciparum*), **crescent/banana-shaped gametocytes** (*P. falciparum*), **Schüffner’s dots** (*P. vivax/ovale*), **Anopheles mosquito**.
- \*\*\*Babesia spp.\*\*\*: **Zoonosis**, **Ixodes ticks**, **tetrad forms/Maltese cross**, **splenectomy** (risk factor for severe disease), **intracellular RBC**, mimics malaria but lacks pigment.
- ***Toxoplasma gondii***: **Cat feces**, **raw meat**, **lymphadenitis**, **CNS ring-enhancing lesions** (AIDS), **congenital infection** (chorioretinitis, hydrocephalus, cerebral calcifications), **cat litter**, **tachyzoites**.
- ***Giardia lamblia***: **Falling-leaf motility**, **old man face/pear shape**, **ventral sucking disk**, **beaver fever**, **steatorrhea** (fatty, foul-smelling diarrhea), **campers/hikers**, **pristine northern lakes**.
- ***Entamoeba histolytica***: **flask-shaped ulcers**, **anchovy paste liver abscess**, **ingested RBCs** (erythrophagocytosis), **wagon-wheel-like nucleus**, **male homosexuals**.
- ***Naegleria fowleri***: **Primary amebic meningoencephalitis (PAM)**, **cribriform plate**, **swimming in warm freshwater**, **rapid progression to death**, ameboid trophozoites in CSF.
- \*\*\*Acanthamoeba spp.\*\*\*: **Granulomatous amebic encephalitis (GAE)**, **keratitis**, **contact lens wearers**, **homemade saline**, **wrinkled cysts**.
- ***Trypanosoma cruzi***: **Chagas disease**, **Reduviid bug (kissing bug)**, **Romaña’s sign** (unilateral periorbital edema), **chagoma**, **megacolon**, **megaesophagus**, **C-shaped trypomastigote**.
- ***Trypanosoma brucei***: **African sleeping sickness**, **Tsetse fly**, **Winterbottom’s sign** (enlarged posterior cervical lymph nodes), **Kerandel’s sign** (delayed pain sensation).
- \*\*\*Leishmania spp.\*\*\*: **Sandfly**, **Kala-azar** (visceral leishmaniasis), **Amastigote (Leishman-Donovan body)**, cutaneous ulcers (**Oriental sore**), hepatosplenomegaly.
**Nematodes (Roundworms)**
- ***Ascaris lumbricoides***: **Giant intestinal roundworm**, **Löffler’s syndrome** (pulmonary phase), **VLM**, **fertilized/unfertilized eggs**, **biliary obstruction** (CBD), "trolley car lines" on X-ray.
- ***Enterobius vermicularis***: **Pinworm/seatworm**, **perianal itching**, **Scotch tape preparation**, **football-shaped eggs** (flattened on one side), children, **retroinfection**.
- ***Strongyloides stercoralis***: **Threadworm**, **autoinfection**, **hyperinfection syndrome** (immunocompromised), **larva currens** (serpiginous track on thighs), **rhabditiform larvae in stool**, **agar plate culture**.
- ***Trichinella spiralis***: **Pork worm**, **encysted larvae in muscle**, **muscle biopsy**, **periorbital edema**, **splinter hemorrhages** (nail beds), **high eosinophilia** (up to 90%), ingestion of **wild game/bear meat**.
- \*\*\*Hookworms (*Necator/Ancylostoma*)\*\*\*: **Iron deficiency anemia**, **ground itch** (larval penetration), **filariform larvae**, **Old World vs. New World**.
- ***Toxocara canis/cati***: **Visceral/Ocular larva migrans (VLM/OLM)**, **retinoblastoma** (misdiagnosis), **pica** (eating dirt), **puppies/kittens**, **dimpled/golf-ball-like egg shell**, **high eosinophilia**.
- ***Angiostrongylus cantonensis***: **Rat lungworm**, **eosinophilic meningitis**, **African giant snail**, **slugs**, **1945 Taiwan** (first case), ingestion of **raw snails or contaminated lettuce**.
- ***Anisakis simplex***: **Herring/cod worm**, **sushi/sashimi**, **tingling-throat syndrome**, **endoscopy** (diagnosis and treatment), **perforated ulcer**, mimics appendicitis.
- ***Gnathostoma spinigerum***: **Migratory swellings**, **creeping eruption**, **head bulb with rows of spines**, **raw freshwater fish/frog/poultry**.
- ***Capillaria philippinensis***: **Intestinal capillariasis**, **raw freshwater fish**, **peanut-shaped eggs** (polar plugs, striated), **autoinfection**, **malabsorption syndrome**, Thailand/Philippines.
- ***Wuchereria bancrofti/Brugia malayi***: **Elephantiasis**, **lymphatic filariasis**, **nocturnal periodicity** (night blood draw), **sheathed microfilariae**, **mosquito vector**, **chyluria**.
- ***Loa loa***: **African eye worm**, **Chrysops fly (mango fly)**, **Calabar swellings** (transient subcutaneous edema), **diurnal periodicity**, crossing the conjunctiva.
- ***Onchocerca volvulus***: **River blindness**, **Simulium (blackfly)**, **skin snips**, **hanging groin**, **Onchocercoma** (nodules), **Leopard skin** (depigmentation).
- ***Dracunculus medinensis***: **Guinea worm**, **"Fiery serpent"**, **Copepod/Cyclops** (intermediate host), **step-wells**, winding worm on a **stick**, **calcified worms on X-ray**.
**Trematodes (Flukes)**
- ***Clonorchis sinensis***: **Chinese liver fluke**, **raw freshwater fish**, **cholangiocarcinoma**, **biliary obstruction**, **operculated egg with shoulders** and **comma-shaped/tiny knob** at posterior.
- ***Fasciola hepatica***: **Sheep liver fluke**, **watercress/aquatic plants**, **"liver rot"** (in sheep), **halzoun syndrome** (ingestion of raw liver), large operculated eggs.
- ***Fasciolopsis buski***: **Giant intestinal fluke**, **water chestnut/bamboo shoots**, pigs/hogs (reservoir), large operculated eggs.
- ***Paragonimus westermani***: **Lung fluke**, **raw crab/crayfish**, **rusty sputum** (iron filings), **mimics tuberculosis**, **coin lesion**.
- \*\*\*Schistosoma spp.\*\*\*: **Snail fever**, **Katayama fever**, **swimmer’s itch**, **cercariae**, **mollusk/snail host**.
  - ***S. mansoni***: **large lateral spine**, stool.
  - ***S. japonicum***: **tiny/minute lateral spine**, stool, **highest egg production**, **CNS involvement/seizures**.
  - ***S. haematobium***: **terminal spine**, **urine**, **bladder cancer** (squamous cell carcinoma), **hematuria**.
**Cestodes (Tapeworms)**
- ***Diphyllobothrium latum***: **Fish tapeworm**, **raw freshwater fish**, **Vitamin B12 deficiency/pernicious anemia**, **broad proglottids (wider than long)**, **uterine rosette**, **operculated egg with abopercular knob**.
- ***Taenia solium***: **Pork tapeworm**, **cysticercosis/neurocysticercosis** (ingestion of eggs), **epileptic seizures**, **calcified cysts** (X-ray/CT), **armed scolex (hooks)**, **7–13 uterine branches**.
- ***Taenia saginata***: **Beef tapeworm**, **unarmed scolex (no hooks)**, **15–20 uterine branches**, very long (up to 12m), proglottids **crawling from anus**.
- ***Echinococcus granulosus***: **Hydatid disease**, **cystic hydatid**, **sheep/dogs**, **hydatid sand**, **daughter cysts**, **anaphylactic shock** (if cyst ruptures), **Water lily sign**.
- ***Hymenolepis nana***: **Dwarf tapeworm**, **polar filaments**, **six-hooked oncosphere**, **most common tapeworm in US**, **no intermediate host required** (direct cycle).
- ***Dipylidium caninum***: **Dog tapeworm/pumpkin seed tapeworm**, **flea ingestion**, **egg packets**, cucumber-seed-like proglottids, **children licked by pets**.
- \*\*\*Spirometra spp.\*\*\*: **Sparganosis**, **plerocercoid**, **frog/snake meat**, **eye poultice** (traditional treatment), **migratory subcutaneous nodules**.
